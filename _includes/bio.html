I am a computer scientist with a research focus on cryptography and work as a Scientist at AIT Austrian Institute of Technology in Vienna, Austria. My interests cover a broad range of topics in cryptography, security and privacy. I am particularly interested in the design of provably secure and practical public key cryptographic primitives and privacy-enhancing protocols, their foundations and their application to provide strong security and privacy guarantees in emerging technologies and computing paradigms (e.g., cloud computing, distributed ledgers or the Internet of Things). Before joining AIT, I held a Senior Researcher position (2015-2017) and a Postdoc position (2012-2015) in the crypto group at IAIK, Graz University of Technology and a Research Associate position (2011-2012) at Carinthia University of Applied Sciences. I hold a PhD degree (Dr.techn.) in computer science from University of Klagenfurt, which I have obtained in 2011 (with distinction). I am a member of the International Association for Cryptologic Research (IACR) and the Austrian Computer Society (OCG).

I am one of the designers of the Picnic family of post-quantum digital signature schemes (see also here), a third-round candidate in NIST's post-quantum crypto standardization project.
